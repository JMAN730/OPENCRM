Editor's Note

Somewhere along the way, reading became a thing we did between other things — a headline glimpsed in a queue, a paragraph abandoned at the lights. We built The Margin as a quiet rebellion against that. Four times a year, on heavy uncoated paper, we publish stories that could not be told quickly, by writers who were given the rarest luxury in this trade: time.

This issue lingers at the edges of things — a harbour town learning to live with the tide, a clockmaker who measures grief in escapements, an economy run entirely on favours. None of it is breaking. All of it, we think, is worth knowing. Pour something. Sit down. The margin is where you do your best thinking; we simply gave it a magazine.

With our compliments, Iris Calloway Founding Editor

Why Slow

We are not in the business of now.

The news will always be faster than us, and we have made our peace with that. What we offer instead is depth: stories reported over seasons, not afternoons, and given room to breathe on the page. No autoplay. No infinite scroll. No urgency manufactured to keep you here a moment longer than the writing earns.

A quarterly arrives, you read it, and then it is yours to keep on a shelf. That is the whole promise. We think it is a generous one.
